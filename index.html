Programme Overview

Built for Sri Lanka's
Growing Sectors

Livestock and Fisheries play a crucial role in the economy of Sri Lanka where the livelihood of many depends on direct or indirect involvement in those sectors.

With the expansion and development of those industries, the need has risen for graduates specialized in those fields — capable of solving problems and boosting growth both locally and internationally.

The B.Sc. AS&F degree was meticulously designed to cater to this growing demand, balancing deep theoretical grounding with extensive practical exposure.

  • 📚 Thorough and well-balanced theoretical knowledge alongside practical skills (Theory : Practical = 1437h : 1286h)
  • 🏭 Series of on-farm and industrial training in all major disciplines, periodically scheduled during semester and vacation periods
  • 🔬 Supplementary minor projects and a research-oriented major project providing in-depth knowledge on a specific field
  • 🎓 Graduates equipped to pursue careers as academics, researchers, managers, planners and entrepreneurs

Four-Year Journey

Academic Roadmap

Year 1
Foundations (Semesters 1 & 2)
Core biochemistry, anatomy, physiology, genetics, nutrition, fish biology, and introductory crop and soil science. Industrial training in animal production and fisheries.
34 credits
Year 2
Applied Sciences (Semesters 3 & 4)
Marine fisheries, animal diseases, shrimp farming, behaviour and welfare, ruminant and poultry production. Optional specialisations begin. 4-week in-plant training.
36 credits + 4wk training
Year 3
Industry & Management (Semesters 5 & 6)
Processing technology, resource management, statistics, livestock legislation, wildlife, dairy and poultry technology, farming systems. 4-week in-plant training.
34 credits + 4wk training
Year 4
Advanced Studies & Research (Semesters 7 & 8)
By-product technology, biotechnology, biosecurity, advanced statistics, livestock economics. Final semester dedicated entirely to an individual research project under senior academic supervision.
24 credits · 6-credit thesis

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the B.Sc. Animal Science and Fisheries degree at the University of Peradeniya?

It is a four-year honours degree programme at the Faculty of Agriculture, University of Peradeniya, Sri Lanka, offering 128 credits in livestock management, aquaculture, and fisheries.

What are the career prospects for Animal Science and Fisheries graduates in Sri Lanka?

Graduates are equipped for careers as academics, researchers, farm managers, planners, and entrepreneurs in the growing local and international livestock and aquaculture industries.
